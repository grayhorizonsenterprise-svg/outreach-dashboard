## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (for Project Pitch — 250 words)

Gray Horizons Enterprise is developing an AI-powered operational intelligence platform that closes the gap between AI adoption and AI effectiveness for small and medium-sized service businesses.

The core problem: 88% of US businesses have adopted AI tools. Only 6% are using them effectively. The gap is not capability — it is implementation. Small businesses lack the technical infrastructure to translate AI potential into operational outcomes: automated lead capture, 24/7 voice intake, systematic follow-up, and real-time performance monitoring.

The innovation: Gray Horizons Enterprise has developed a proprietary scoring and automation architecture that combines conversational AI (voice agents), CRM workflow automation, and real-time signal intelligence into a single managed system deployable for any service-sector business in under 7 days. The system includes a novel application of Kelly Criterion position-sizing logic to business lead scoring — ranking inbound leads by conversion probability and automating response priority accordingly.

The market: 33 million US small businesses, 60% of which operate in service sectors with acute lead-handling problems. The agentic AI market is projected at $202 billion by 2026. No current platform delivers a full-stack, managed AI OS to businesses under $5M in annual revenue at accessible price points.

The ask: NSF SBIR Phase I funding of $275,000 to validate the core technical claims, formalize the lead-scoring algorithm, and deploy to a cohort of 25 service businesses with documented outcomes.

## INNOVATION SECTION
*(Technical merit — this is what NSF cares about most)*

**What is the technical innovation?**

The Gray Horizons Enterprise platform combines three technical components into a unified operational AI system:

**1. Probabilistic Lead Scoring Engine**
Standard CRM systems rank leads by status (new, contacted, qualified). The GHE system ranks leads by conversion probability — a real-time score derived from response speed, message content analysis, and historical close-rate data for similar business types. This score drives automated response priority: high-probability leads receive immediate AI voice outreach; lower-probability leads enter a systematic nurture sequence. The algorithm is a novel application of Kelly Criterion fractional sizing logic (originally developed for gambling and financial markets) to sales funnel management.

**2. Autonomous Voice Intake Agent**
The GHE voice agent uses conversational AI to handle 100% of inbound calls for service businesses — qualifying leads, capturing job details, and booking calendar appointments without human involvement. The agent uses a dynamic script that adapts to caller responses, applies sentiment detection to flag distressed or urgent callers for immediate human escalation, and logs full transcripts for quality and compliance review. Current deployments demonstrate sub-2-minute average handle time for full lead qualification and booking.

**3. Closed-Loop Signal Intelligence (Edge Engine)**
The Edge Engine applies institutional-grade market analysis to retail-accessible data: RSI divergence, volume anomaly detection (triggering at 2x-3x 30-day average), EMA crossover confirmation, and congressional disclosure tracking under the STOCK Act. The system produces a composite 0-100 score and applies Kelly Criterion fractional position sizing so subscribers receive not just a signal but a specific recommended exposure level. This represents a novel synthesis of public legislative disclosure data with technical market indicators.

**What is the research question?**

Can a unified AI operational system — combining lead scoring, autonomous voice intake, and signal intelligence — be validated as statistically superior to manual operations for service-sector SMBs, with measurable outcomes in lead conversion rate, response time, and revenue per inbound contact?

**What is the R&D plan?**

Phase I (12 months):
- Month 1-3: Formalize lead-scoring algorithm; establish measurement baseline with pilot cohort of 10 service businesses
- Month 4-8: Deploy AI voice agent to all 10 pilot businesses; collect call-handling, conversion, and booking data
- Month 9-12: Analyze outcomes vs. control group (businesses without GHE system); document statistical significance of improvements; prepare Phase II proposal

## BROADER IMPACT

The commercialization path is direct: a validated, documented system with proven outcomes converts immediately into recurring $750/month retainer contracts. The Phase I cohort of 25 businesses generates $18,750 MRR at 100% conversion.

The societal impact is broader: service-sector businesses are disproportionately minority-owned, first-generation, and capital-constrained. Giving them access to AI infrastructure that was previously available only to enterprise companies closes a compounding inequality — more revenue, more jobs, more community investment.

NSF's broader impact criteria explicitly include "increasing the participation of underrepresented groups" and "improving economic competitiveness." Gray Horizons Enterprise addresses both directly: a Black-founded company building AI infrastructure for the underserved small business market.
