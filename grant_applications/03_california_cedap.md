## PROJECT DESCRIPTION
*(What will the grant funds support? 250-400 words — paste directly)*

Gray Horizons Enterprise is requesting CEDAP funding to accelerate the deployment of AI automation systems for underserved local service businesses in California's Inland Empire region and surrounding communities.

The project has two components.

The first is client acquisition and service delivery infrastructure. Gray Horizons Enterprise has built a complete AI automation stack including GoHighLevel CRM workflows, AI voice agents for inbound call handling, and automated lead nurture sequences. The CEDAP grant funds the critical 90-day window required to close the first three paying retainer clients, document the outcomes, and establish the case studies needed to scale. Without this bridge, the technology sits ready while the founder works against a survival timeline that compresses every sales conversation.

The second component is community impact: the businesses we serve are disproportionately minority-owned, working-class operations in trades industries — HVAC, roofing, plumbing, contracting, landscaping. These are the businesses that keep Inland Empire communities running. They are also the ones being left behind by a technology shift that assumes you have either venture capital or an in-house engineering team to compete.

Gray Horizons Enterprise exists precisely because neither of those options is available to most small business owners in this region. We bring the same AI capabilities that enterprise companies deploy at $150,000 per year in salary costs, and we deliver them for $750 per month. The CEDAP grant accelerates the point at which we can prove this model works — and then replicate it across the Inland Empire's small business ecosystem.

## COMMUNITY IMPACT STATEMENT
*(If the application asks about community benefit — paste directly)*

California's Inland Empire has one of the highest concentrations of small, minority-owned service businesses in the state — and one of the lowest rates of technology adoption among those businesses.

Gray Horizons Enterprise is owned and operated by a Black entrepreneur who grew up in the communities these businesses serve. Every client we close is a business that now competes more effectively, books more jobs, loses fewer leads, and generates more revenue to pay wages in the local economy.

The model is not charity. It is economic leverage. AI automation creates the same productivity gain for a $500,000 revenue plumbing company that a $5 million enterprise gets from a dedicated IT department. The difference is that Gray Horizons Enterprise makes that leverage available at 1/200th of the cost.

The CEDAP grant does not fund a speculative idea. It funds a working technology deployed by a California business owner to create measurable economic impact for the underserved businesses this program was designed to support.
